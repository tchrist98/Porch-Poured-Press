About

Tara Christian grew up in a small town in Alabama, where close-knit communities and deeply rooted family traditions shaped her understanding of what it means to belong. In a place where life is messy, real, and everyone has a story worth telling.

When she isn't writing, you'll find Tara creating something with her hands, tending her garden, and spending time with family — pursuits she affectionately has dubbed as "grandma-ish" and embraces wholeheartedly. She also is probably lovingly negotiating peace with her dog Cash.

About Saylor

Saylor Delahaye writes dark romance for the girls who want to dance on the bar but never will. For the ones who've imagined the motorcycle, the bad decision, the man they absolutely should not want.

Dark romance is where she goes to be unfiltered — to explore the messy, complicated, morally gray parts of herself and the world that don't fit anywhere else. She has a particular soft spot for morally gray men. Not in spite of their darkness, because of it. A man who owns his shadows doesn't hide them. He shows you exactly who he is and lets you decide.

She believes that showing someone your worst and having them stay anyway is one of the most profound things two people can do. Her books live in that space — between right and wrong, safe and dangerous, too much and not enough. The world is not black and white. Neither are her characters.
